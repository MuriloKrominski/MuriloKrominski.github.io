VeritAI - Chat with Video

VeritAI - Chat with Video is an AI-powered chatbot designed to interact with users by extracting information directly from YouTube videos. The project provides an intuitive and functional interface that leverages advanced language models to analyze and interpret video content, generating accurate and context-aware responses based on the audiovisual data provided by the user.

Developed in Python, VeritAI utilizes robust libraries such as Streamlit (for user interface), LangChain (for building conversational AI workflows), OpenCV (for video processing and visual analysis), and youtube_transcript_api (for audio transcript extraction). Model = llama-3.1-70b-versatile

The code is modular and well-documented, enabling easy future expansions and the integration of additional features, such as subtitle analysis, object recognition, and in-depth insights into video content.

VeritAI - Chat with Docs

VeritAI - Chat with Docs is an intelligent chatbot designed to interact with users by leveraging information extracted from PDF files. With a user-friendly and streamlined interface, the system uses advanced language models to provide responses based on the content of user-uploaded PDFs.

SimuChaos: Gym

The SimuChaos: Gym is a simulator that examines how small behavioral patterns in shared environments can create significant system-wide impacts. Using Monte Carlo simulations, it analyzes the balance between organized and disorganized gym-goers in the Free Weight section of Smart Fit gyms. The project quantifies the effects of disorganization, providing a clear understanding of how individual actions can influence collective experiences.

Based on the real infrastructure of Smart Fit, the largest gym chain in Latin America, SimuChaos-Gym offers practical scenarios and actionable results. Beyond identifying problems, it proposes solutions to reduce the impacts of disorder. Whether you’re a gym manager, a behavioral researcher, or simply curious, this project delivers valuable and applicable insights.

More than just a study on gyms, SimuChaos-Gym is a tool to improve any shared environment. Its findings demonstrate how simple interventions can foster organization and harmony, making it relevant for public spaces, logistics, and other systems. Explore how data can turn chaos into efficiency and positively transform collective experiences.

Remember: A Link to the Past

Remember: A Link to the Past allows companies to "go back in time" and review past activities. It automatically captures screenshots at set intervals, processes images with AI to identify open applications, transcribe text, and generate detailed metadata.

The information is stored in a vectorized database, enabling fast and accurate searches. With an intuitive interface, the system supports semantic searches to locate specific events, documents, or details from past moments.

Ideal for companies of all sizes, Remember centralizes all essential information, boosting efficiency and ensuring security with local storage. It's a practical, cost-effective solution to protect data and optimize productivity.

Amazon Bestsellers Analysis App

Amazon Bestsellers Analysis App is an interactive web application in Python, built with Streamlit, Pandas, Plotly, Scikit-learn, WordCloud, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. Based on the 100 most-read Amazon books from 1947 to 2023, this tool provides in-depth analysis of literary trends, including prices, ratings, and genre popularity.

With interactive charts, the app explores the relationship between price and ranking, top authors, and genre distribution. Additional features, like word clouds and cluster segmentation, offer a deep dive into Amazon's bestsellers.

Created for academic purposes, the Amazon Bestsellers Analysis App also highlights Streamlit’s capabilities and limitations. The application enabled smooth integration with data libraries (and is set for testing with machine learning), despite the noted constraints in multithreading, parallelism, and some layout limitations. Recommended for small to medium web applications without multi-user requirements.

PyCalc

PyCalc is a groundbreaking platform that combines artificial intelligence, natural language processing (NLP), and interactive visualizations to transform how mathematics is learned and applied. Built with a modular architecture using technologies like SymPy, FastAPI, and LLama 3, PyCalc delivers precision and flexibility in symbolic computation, numerical operations, and equation solving, catering to the needs of students, educators, and professionals.

With NLP integration, users can interact naturally, asking questions like "What is the derivative of \(x^2 + 3x\)?" and receiving detailed, tailored responses. Its RESTful APIs ensure seamless integration with educational platforms and corporate tools, while features such as LaTeX rendering, 2D/3D interactive graphs, and collaborative modes create a powerful and accessible environment for teaching, research, and innovation.

More than a calculator, modular and expandable, PyCalc supports personalized learning, automated quizzes, interactive histories, and real-time visual simulations. Its hybrid architecture blends symbolic and numerical computation to handle everything from basic algebra to complex differential equations. Whether in the classroom, the lab, or major projects, PyCalc provides an intuitive, technical, and collaborative mathematical experience.

Game: Rock-Paper-Scissors-Lizard-Spock

"Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock" invites you to dive into the geekiest universe and face off against none other than Sheldon Cooper! Inspired by the legendary game from The Big Bang Theory, this Python project with a simple Tkinter interface lets you test your wits (or luck) in an epic battle of logic and randomness. Can you outplay the master of reason? It’s time to find out.

The rules are clever yet wonderfully chaotic. Each element beats two others: Scissors cuts Paper, Paper covers Rock, Rock crushes Lizard, Lizard poisons Spock, Spock smashes Scissors, Scissors decapitates Lizard, Lizard eats Paper, Paper disproves Spock, and Spock vaporizes Rock. Master these interactions, and you just might have a shot at defeating Sheldon—or at least confusing him.

Each move is a blend of strategy and serendipity. Will you outthink Sheldon, or will your choice of Lizard lead to an unexpected triumph? Either way, the fun is in the challenge, and the satisfaction of besting Spock (and Sheldon) is unmatched. But be warned: Sheldon never forgets a win—or a loss.🖖
